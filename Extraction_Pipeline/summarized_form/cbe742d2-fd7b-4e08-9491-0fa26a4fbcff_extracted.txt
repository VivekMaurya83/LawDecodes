Please note that your act of occupying or claiming rights over the said property is in violation of the following legal provisions:

Section 441, Indian Penal Code (IPC) – Criminal Trespass
Entering or remaining unlawfully in property belonging to another person with the intent to intimidate, insult, or annoy the rightful owner amounts to criminal trespass.

Section 420, IPC – Cheating and Dishonestly Inducing Delivery of Property
Making false representations or claiming ownership of property through fraudulent means is a punishable offence.

Sections 467 and 468, IPC – Forgery of Valuable Security and Fraudulent Documents
Any attempt to forge or fabricate documents to claim ownership of property attracts severe penal consequences, including imprisonment.

Section 6, Transfer of Property Act, 1882
Property cannot be transferred unlawfully or by persons not authorized to do so.

Section 52, Transfer of Property Act, 1882 – Doctrine of Lis Pendens
Any transaction or transfer made while the property is under legal dispute is void and unenforceable.

Therefore, you are hereby directed to immediately cease all activities and claims related to the said property and vacate the premises within 15 (fifteen) days of receipt of this notice.

Failure to comply shall compel the undersigned to initiate appropriate civil and criminal proceedings before the competent court of law, including but not limited to filing suits under the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, and criminal prosecution under relevant provisions of the Indian Penal Code.

You are further warned not to sell, lease, or create any encumbrance upon the said property, as such actions shall be deemed null and void and will attract legal consequences.

This notice is issued without prejudice to the lawful rights and remedies available to the undersigned under Indian law.
